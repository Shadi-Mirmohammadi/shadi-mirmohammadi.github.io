    Custom Instrumentation for Chiroptical Spectroscopy

    A 3D-printed sample fixture for the CD spectrometer, designed in Fusion 360 — and the chiral metasurface signal it made reproducible.

    Shadi Mirmohammadi — Tang Lab, University of Utah

    The problem: a measurement that moves when the sample does

    Circular dichroism (CD) measures the difference in absorption between left- and right-circularly polarized light — for a chiral metasurface, a signal of a few millidegrees riding on a much larger absorbance. At that scale, where the substrate sits in the beam matters: hand-placing a coated fused-silica chip in the spectrometer’s sample compartment shifts and reshapes the spectrum from scan to scan, making successive measurements of the same sample hard to compare.

    The fix: design the sample’s seat

    So I designed the seat. Two parts, modeled in Fusion 360 and 3D-printed: a rail mount that slides into the Jasco spectrometer’s sample compartment at a fixed position along the beam path, and a square substrate holder — a recessed pocket with corner mounting holes that seats the chip at the same position and orientation every time. The holder took six design iterations to get right.

    The payoff: a clean bisignate signal — and proof the fixture matters

    With the fixture in place, the diarylethene (DAE) chiral metasurface shows a clear bisignate CD couplet across ~580–615 nm: a negative lobe reaching −10.5 mdeg near 580 nm and a positive lobe of +14.3 mdeg near 610 nm — roughly 10–15× above the ±1 mdeg noise floor measured on air and bare fused-silica references, so the chiroptical response is real, not instrumental.

    And the position comparison doubles as the fixture’s own justification: sliding the sample from mid-beam toward the detector visibly weakens and shifts the couplet. That placement sensitivity is precisely why a reproducible seat is worth six CAD revisions — in precision optics, the boring mechanical part is often what makes the exciting measurement believable.

    Chiral metasurface sample from Tang-lab collaborators; CD measurements on the lab’s Jasco spectropolarimeter.
