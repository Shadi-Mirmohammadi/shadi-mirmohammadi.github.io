    λ = 1 mm · 300 GHz · terahertz

    Optically Tunable THz Metasurfaces & Transparent Conductors

    THz photonics · Ansys HFSS · Cleanroom

    How do you switch terahertz waves with light? A metasurface on Fe-doped β-Ga2O3, designed with full-wave 3D EM simulation, flips from near-perfect absorber to mirror at 300 GHz under 808 nm pumping (95% reflected-power modulation) — backed by sputtered ITO transparent electrodes whose THz-frequency sheet resistance is extracted by CW-THz photomixing and reconciled with DC four-point probe.

    View project (with interactive demo) → IRMMW-THz 2026 · accepted
    λ = 808 nm · near-infrared

    10 kV β-Ga2O3 Photoconductive Switches

    Pulsed power · UWBG semiconductors

    Can you switch 10,000 volts with a near-infrared laser? Fe impurity states in β-Ga2O3 enable 808 nm sub-bandgap triggering of a 10 kV switch — on/off ratio >15 — while mapping the electrothermal stability trade-off.

    View project → Manuscript · ready to submit
    λ = 0.35 mm · 0.87 THz

    EIT-Like Terahertz Metasurface: a Full-Wave Design Study

    Ansys HFSS · Coupled resonators · Slow light

    Two coupled resonators, one engineered transparency window. Reproducing a published EIT terahertz metasurface in HFSS to within 0.7% on the fundamental dip — then asking the fabrication questions the paper didn't: how thin can the metal go (skin depth says 84 nm, the paper used 200), and what does a 17×-lower-loss substrate buy? Every simulated point is distilled into coupled-resonator physics (γ₁, γ₂, κ, δ) by a global-optimizer fit.

    View project (with slow-light demo & talk) → Report ↗
    λ = 236–270 nm → 612 nm · deep UV in, red out

    Excitation-Switchable Eu³⁺ Luminescence in GdScO₃

    Photoluminescence · Optical metrology

    A nominally undoped perovskite substrate that changes emission color on command: ~13 nm of deep-UV tuning flips trace-Eu³⁺ (1–2 ppm) emission from blue-green to red (>10× ratio collapse), and annealing erases the defect band — a nondestructive optical screen for scandate substrates.

    View project (with interactive data) → Opt. Mater. Express · ready to submit
    λ = 233–267 nm · deep ultraviolet

    UV Photoresponsivity of GeO2 Photodetectors

    UWBG semiconductors · Collaboration

    How responsive are ultrawide-bandgap GeO2 detectors to UV light, really? Mapping the solar-blind spectral response (233–267 nm) with a tunable third-harmonic laser source — with Dr. Kai Fu's lab (Imteaz Rahaman) and Prof. Steve Blair.

    ν = 9.5 GHz · microwave

    Engineering Spin States in Si QD–Organic Hybrids

    Quantum information · TR-EPR

    Can chemistry program a quantum dot's spin? Time-resolved EPR shows hydrosilylation-functionalized Si QD–anthracene hybrids receive their triplet already spin-polarized — with the linker chemistry setting the triplet's delocalization. With Argonne National Lab.
