    Optically Tunable THz Metasurface Absorber

    An optically reconfigurable terahertz metasurface on Fe-doped β-Ga₂O₃ — IRMMW-THz 2026 (accepted).

    λ = 1 mm · 300 GHz · terahertz

    95% of terahertz power, switched with light.

    IRMMW-THz 2026 · accepted

    Seyedeh Shadi Mirmohammadi and Berardi Sensale-Rodriguez Department of Electrical & Computer Engineering, University of Utah

    Accepted at the 51st International Conference on Infrared, Millimeter, and Terahertz Waves (IRMMW-THz 2026), Salt Lake City, October 2026.

    Abstract

    We present an optically tunable terahertz (THz) metasurface absorber based on Fe-doped β-Ga2O3, an ultrawide-bandgap semiconductor (Eg ≈ 4.8 eV). Despite this large bandgap, Fe acceptor states enable sub-bandgap photoexcitation at 808 nm, producing pump-power-dependent photoconductivity. Full-wave electromagnetic simulations for σ = 0 to 25 S/m show 82.2% absorption modulation and 95.0% reflected-power modulation at 300 GHz, establishing Fe:β-Ga2O3 as a promising platform for optically reconfigurable THz photonics.

    Introduction

    β-Ga2O3 is an ultrawide-bandgap semiconductor of interest for power electronics and UV photonics. In Fe-doped β-Ga2O3, mid-gap Fe acceptor states lie approximately 0.85 eV below the conduction band. Under sub-bandgap 808 nm continuous-wave illumination, electrons are excited from these acceptors into the conduction band, producing THz photoconductivity that increases with pump power [1]. Jiang et al. reported photoinduced conductivities up to ~25 S/m at 2 W pump power [1]. This response motivates THz metasurfaces that use photoinduced conductivity for contact-free tuning without electrical contacts, gates, or temperature control.

    Device Design

    The device consists of a gold H-shaped split-ring resonator (SRR) array on a 724 μm thick Fe:β-Ga2O3 substrate backed by a continuous gold ground plane. The unit-cell parameters are P = 150 μm, a = 120 μm, g = 10 μm, w = 15 μm, and Au thickness 0.2 μm. The resonator topology is adapted from the SRR design in [2] for 300 GHz operation on Fe:β-Ga2O3. The SRR geometry provides a strong inductive-capacitive resonance near 300 GHz; with the ground plane blocking transmission, the incident THz power is partitioned only between reflection and absorption. Full-wave simulations were performed in ANSYS HFSS 2025 R2 with adaptive meshing at 0.32 THz (20 passes, ΔS < 0.01) and a 280 to 320 GHz sweep over 12 conductivity values (σ = 0 to 25 S/m).

    Results

    In the dark state (σ = 0), the device reaches 95.8% absorption at 300.4 GHz, owed to ohmic losses in the finite-conductivity metals. As σ in the Fe-doped β-Ga2O3 increases toward the pump-limited value of ~25 S/m [1], the resonator becomes progressively overcoupled: absorption decreases to 17.0%, while reflected power increases from 4.2% to 83.0%. The corresponding absorption modulation is 82.2%; the reflected-power modulation, defined between the dark and illuminated states, is 95.0%. Since the ground plane suppresses transmission for all σ, the device operates as an optically controlled reflection-mode THz modulator.

    The full simulation dataset — every S-parameter at every one of the 12 conductivity states — tells the switching story at a glance. In the dark, a single bright stripe of near-total absorption sits at 300.4 GHz; turning up the illumination doesn’t shift it, it erases it. Cutting through the map at the resonance gives the device’s transfer curve: absorption and reflection trade places over roughly two decades of photoconductivity, with most of the switching happening between 0.5 and 10 S/m — comfortably inside the range an 808 nm pump can reach.

    Summary

    Full-wave simulations show that a gold SRR metasurface on Fe:β-Ga2O3 can achieve 82.2% absorption modulation and 95.0% reflected-power modulation at 300 GHz under 808 nm optical pumping. The combination of ultrawide-bandgap stability and optically accessible Fe mid-gap states makes this platform promising for contact-free, optically reconfigurable THz photonic devices. Experimental validation is currently underway.

    The experimental side: transparent conductors at THz frequencies

    Making this device real raises a materials question of its own: can a transparent electrode stay conductive at terahertz frequencies? Toward experimental validation, I develop sputtered ITO thin films on Ga2O3 and sapphire as transparent conductors — films that must pass the 808 nm pump light while conducting at 300 GHz. I extract their THz-frequency sheet resistance by CW-THz photomixing spectroscopy (Toptica) and reconcile it against DC four-point-probe measurements — because a film that looks conductive to a multimeter can behave very differently at 0.3 THz.

    The first films are measured. Comparing the same sapphire piece before and after sputtering ~160 nm of ITO, the Fabry–Pérot fringe peaks tell two stories at once: the fringe spacing (~114 GHz) is a built-in thickness gauge — it pins the substrate at ~0.43 mm — and the fringe amplitude reports the film’s conductivity, with peak transmission dropping from a mean of 0.95 (bare) to 0.44 (coated):

    Choosing the ITO thickness

    On the design side, an HFSS sweep answers how thick the ITO electrode should be: extinction ratio and insertion loss both improve with thickness, crossing the 10 dB extinction target well before the optical-transparency budget runs out. 500 nm is the sweet spot (extinction ratio ≈ 16–17 dB for TE/TM with the lowest insertion loss, ≈ 3.9–4.2 dB), with 380 nm held as a fabrication-friendly backup:
